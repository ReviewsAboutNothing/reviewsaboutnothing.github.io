The Rise and Fall of a Midwest Princess (2023)

Chappell Roan

Chappell Roan has suddenly risen to popularity, so why don’t we talk about her famous debut album?

The Rise and Fall of a Midwest Princess is a pop album that varies between high energy anthems, and heartfelt ballads. It highlights themes of passion, such as in ‘Naked In Manhattan’, being part of the LGBTQ+ community, like in ‘Pink Pony Club’ and the struggles of love, such as in ‘Coffee’

‘Red Wine Supernova’ is one of the best songs on the album. Its toned down, but catchy chorus contrasts the fast and energetic verses and bridge, which is great sonically.

However, ‘Picture You’ is one of the songs which doesn’t fit as well, due to it being in the middle of a three-track-long decrease in energy. Unfortunately, it sounds too weary which sticks out from the rest of the album.

Chappell is a great lyricist, as she constantly uses symbolic metaphors to describe her passion for her lover. This is largely seen in the song ‘Kaleidoscope’, where she compares love to a kaleidoscope’s shapes and colours.

I would heavily recommend this album for many. This is for its honest storytelling, especially in the ballads, and danceable production, especially in the anthems.
